Rhinoplasty to rotate tip of nose downwards?
Rotating the tip downward in Asian rhinoplasty
Dec 10, 2014
Asian patients seeking rhinoplasty often desire a more narrow, refined, and projected tip. The tip may also be turned up or overrotated such that nostril show on the frontal view is excessive. Tip grafting can turn down or counterrotate the nose and reduce nostril visibility.

How long does an Asian rhinoplasty last that requires autologous cartilage graft?
Durability of grafts in Asian rhinoplasty
Dec 10, 2014
A large volume of grafting material is often required in Asian rhinoplasty to raise the bridge and refine the tip. Enough septal cartilage is rarely available and rib grafting is often used. There is virtually no resorption of rib cartilage over time so it is essentially permanent.

Lengthening the nose in revision rhinoplasty
Dec 7, 2014
Narrowing the nasal bones may slightly increase the height of the bridge but it will not lengthen the nose. Weight loss after rhinoplasty
Sep 4, 2014
Drastic weight loss changes the shape and size of the face and thus alters the balance with the nose. For example, a wider nose will look more proportional on a wider, rounder face but may appear too large on a slimmer face.
During Septoplasty I have lost my septum tip. My nose septum is goes down. What procedure will
Complications from septoplasty
Sep 4, 2014
Overaggressive excision of septal cartilage during a septoplasty can weaken the nose and lead to both functional and cosmetic complications. If too much cartilage is excised from the most forward, or caudal, portion of the septum, the nasal tip will begin to droop. If too much cartilage is removed from the central portion of the septum, the bridge will begin to collapse and create a saddle nose. Correction requires rebuilding the septum with cartilage grafts.
