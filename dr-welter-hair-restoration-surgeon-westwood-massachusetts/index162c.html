Statement: Dr. Welter is an accomplished Cosmetic and Hair Restoration Surgeon with research focusing on regenerative medicine technology and particularly in its ability to improve cosmetic outcomes and prolong lifespan and healthspan simultaneously.

Dr. Welter completed his residency at Brown University and obtained his medical degree from the University of Oklahoma. He also earned a PhD in Biochemistry and Molecular Biology from Oklahoma State University where he researched the effects of nitric oxide on electron transport complexes and he has an extensive background in metabolic processes.

Upon completion of training, Dr. Welter went into private practice in Massachusetts. He quickly developed a network of primary care and urgent care centers from the ground up and was one of the first adopters of the outpatient urgent care model.

In 2005 he specialized in hair restoration and founded the New England Center for Hair Restoration. He was one of the first adopters of cellular therapy for hair with the use of activated platelet rich plasma in 2006. And in 2011 he began the use first stromal vascular fraction treatment for hair loss using autologous derived stem cells to stimulate the follicular niche to progress to anagen (essentially getting weaker hairs to strengthen to protect them from further hair loss). This approach to hair loss continues to be researched and applied clinically today with increasing promise.

His background in biochemistry and molecular biology has placed him in a unique position as he merged his clinical practice with his interests in regenerative medicine and cosmetic surgery, allowing him to explore clinical applications for his patients that might otherwise be unavailable and leading him to found Regeneris Medical which focuses on cell based therapies, longevity, health, wellness and beauty.

He has had the pleasure of being mentored by some of the top physicians and scientists in the longevity, cosmetic and regenerative medicine landscapes and he remains committed to compiling the best evidence based approach to his practice to help as many people as possible live their longest, best, most beautiful life.

How Long Does Minoxidil 5 Take to Work?
Regrowth with Minoxidil
Nov 12, 2018
Because of the speed at which human hair grows, you'll want to wait at least 6 months before making a determination as to how it's working for you.  Keep in mind, however, that "fully regrow" may not be a realistic expectation.  Because hair loss is typically progressive, if your hair looks "the same or better" after 1 year, that's technically improvement over no treatment at all.  It doesn't mean it's not worth doing, it just means you'll want to have realistic expectations going in.
What are the risks of a woman using men's Rogaine?
.Men's vs Women's Rogaine
Nov 12, 2018
Many doctors recommend the 5% strength for men AND Women.  There are a number of treatment options, and Minoxidil (the generic name for Rogaine) is one of the least expensive ones and is considered safe.  There are several possible causes for female hair loss, so it's very important to try to identify the underlying cause.
How many SMP sessions are needed?
Sessions of SMP
Nov 10, 2018
We always do three sessions scheduled about 2 weeks apart.  This allows for the best grading and distribution of the pigment which gives better aesthetic results.
