Is there a need for cartilage support in all nose jobs?
Nasal support with rhinoplasty surgery
Jun 13, 2018
Generally when performing Open (External) Rhinoplasty, some type of support is necessary to preserve tip position that being either a cartilage strut or suturing of the columella to the septum.
With Closed (endonasal) Rhinoplasty cartilage tip support is not affected as much. Cartilage grafts for support are therefore usually not necessary.

How can my crooked tip be fixed?
Crooked nasal tip
May 31, 2018
Generally when addressing a crooked nasal tip I would recommend an external (open) rhinoplasty approach to properly analyze and surgically manage the asymmetry. This may involve simply repositioning and suturing the tip cartilages in a symmetrical fashion but also may involve partial resection of one or both cartilages.

Rhinoplasty will not address any sinus issues however sinus surgery, if necessary to manage chronic recurrent sinusitis, could be performed at the same time.
