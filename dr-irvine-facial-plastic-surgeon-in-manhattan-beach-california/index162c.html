Can I still get a rhinoplasty if I have glaucoma?
Rhinoplasty and glaucoma
Oct 21, 2018
I am glad to hear that your glaucoma is well controlled. Prior to any rhinoplasty procedure, I would recommend getting clearance from your ophthalmologist, in addition to notifying your surgeon. Eye injury during rhinoplasty is extremely rare, and the risk of ocular complications depends on the specific rhinoplasty techniques used.
Best wishes,
Leslie Irvine, M.D.
How can the small bump on one side of nose after rhinoplasty be fixed?
Bump after rhinoplasty
Oct 21, 2018
It is somewhat difficult to tell without examining you in person, but you could have a small nasal tip bossae, caused by buckling or shifting of nasal tip cartilages as the skin heals and “shrink-wraps” around the nose. I would recommend seeing a facial plastic surgeon so that she or he can feel the tip cartilages to better assess it and decide next steps, which may be a revision surgery vs. injections vs. no treatment.
Best wishes,
Leslie Irvine, M.D.

How Long Does a Facelift Last?
Facelift longevity
Aug 22, 2017
For most people, a facelift lasts 5-10 years. This depends on many factors such as skin quality and elasticity, genetics, environmental stressors, and the type of lift that was performed.
The aging process continues after a facelift, but it restarts from an earlier set point, so you should hopefully always look at least 5 to 10 years younger than your actual age.

Facelift Recovery
Facelift Recovery
Aug 17, 2017
I generally tell patients to take 2 weeks off of work and activities for facelift recovery. Some patients with long hair to cover their incisions are able to go back to work after 7 days, but recovery depends on your skin type, other underlying health factors, and what type of surgery was done.
If it is okay with your surgeon and deemed safe for you, hyperbaric oxygen can also help with post-facelift swelling and healing, and I recommend arnica for bruising.
