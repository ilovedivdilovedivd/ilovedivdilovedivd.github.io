Would I be able to get a rhinoplasty if I’m taking a low dosage of diclofenac?
Would I be able to get a rhinoplasty if I’m taking a low dosage of diclofenac?
Oct 19, 2018
You are correct, NSAIDs are typically not recommended when undergoing facial cosmetic surgery.  Specifically for rhinoplasty surgery, NSAIDs will increase intraoperative bleeding, which will subsequently lead to more swelling/bruising and a prolonged recovery.  This excessive swelling may also not completely resolve leading to issues with your final result.  I suggest you speak to your primary physician, to discuss other pain control options.  Good Luck!
Would a tip plasty be a better choice for me than a open rhinoplasty?
Tip Plasty vs Full Rhinoplasty
Oct 15, 2018
The model’s image that you have provided displays both dorsal (nasal bridge) refinement, in addition to tip refinement.  If you desire “thinning” of the dorsum, as well as contour improvement, a more comprehensive rhinoplasty will be required.  A “full” rhinoplasty would include tip, as well as dorsal modifications, to help you achieve your aesthetic goals.  I suggest you followup with a surgeon specializing in rhinoplasty to discuss your goals and review realistic expectations.  Good Luck!
How deviated does a septum have to be to influence the final result of a rhinoplasty?
Septoplasty and Rhinoplasty
Oct 15, 2018
Septoplasty is very commonly performed with rhinoplasty, as nasal form as well as function are typically addressed.  Even when septal deviation is not present, septal cartilage is routinely harvested during rhinoplasty for grafting purposes.  There should be no reason to harvest conchal cartilage (“ear cartilage”), if septal cartilage is available.  I suggest you consult with a surgeon who specializes in rhinoplasty to further discuss your options.  Good Luck!
