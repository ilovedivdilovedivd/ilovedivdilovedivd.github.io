Drastic Rhinoplasty

There is no Drastic Rhinoplasty

The changes you describe are quite standard and would not be considered drastic by any means. The desired outcome of a procedure is a very personal choice.

What one person thinks is drastic might be considered conservative by another. Most surgeries are easy to accomplish in a single procedure.

That’s not to say that a small percentage of patients will request revision surgery. However, planning two separate stages is not common practice.

If a surgeon recommended this to you, I would be cautious. (Richard W. Westreich, MD, Manhattan Facial Plastic Surgeon)

Your requests for improvement of your nose are quite common. But even in the best hands, there is about a 5-15% revision rate. (Steven Wallach, MD, Manhattan Plastic Surgeon)

Rhinoplasty Surgery may be tailored for your specific needs and you could see dramatic results.

I read your concerns. In my practice, some of my patients require dramatic changes in the appearance of their noses, and some require less.

It depends on what you’re starting with and where you want to go. In general, patients with very large, long noses with droopy, bulbous tips will see the most dramatic change after Rhinoplasty Surgery.

Computer-imaging may be helpful for you to determine what to expect after well-performed Rhinoplasty Surgery.

Drastic Rhinoplasty operation

Rhinoplasty Surgery is not staged over several procedures. (Eric M. Joseph, MD, West Orange Facial Plastic Surgeon)

Even a drastic rhinoplasty is a single procedure

Rhinoplasty is often a procedure of small changes to enhance the characteristics of the nose, though sometimes ‘drastic’ changes are needed to reach the goal of the procedure.

Even with very large hump reduction, correction of a severely twisted nose, thinning a very full tip, rhinoplasty is a single procedure tailored to the task at hand, rather than a ‘staged’ procedure. Even the worst nose can be corrected in a single step. (Peter E. Johnson, MD, Chicago Plastic Surgeon)

“Drastic” rhinoplasty does NOT require more than 1 operation!

Drastic Rhinoplasty surgery

Whatever changes your nose requires to achieve the results you are seeking are best accomplished in as few operations as possible! In most cases this is ONE operation, but every surgeon who does nose surgery has occasional patients who request or require additional surgery.

PLANNING in advance for more than one procedure is generally NOT recommended, though I sometimes will do all of the changes that a patient requests EXCEPT for the Weir excisions (at the side and base of each nostril) to reduce lower nasal width and nostril size. The latter can easily be performed as a separate procedure under local anesthesia if either the surgeon or the patient (or both) are not sure if the decrease in nostril size or lower nasal width is really necessary after all of the other requested changes in the rest of the nose.

These require that all the work be done when the patient is asleep (or sedated) and the nasal skin is elevated from the underlying support cartilages and bony structures. Patients sometimes worry about what happens to all of the “extra” skin after the nasal hump is removed.
