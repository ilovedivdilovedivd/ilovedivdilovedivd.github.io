Would I be a candidate for liquid rhinoplasty?
Rhinoplasty for a bump…
Dec 21, 2020
Using fillers to hide a bump is certainly an option – the effect usually lasts 6-8 months and people like it because there is no recovery. Obviously, a rhinoplasty will give you a permanent result, but it is a lot more expensive and 1-2 weeks of recovery is needed. You should consult with a rhinoplasty expert to get the best opinion.
Can the nose tip be lifted by stitching tip to the septum?
Lifting the nasal tip…
Dec 21, 2020
The answer is that it can help, but is usually not the only thing that needs to be done. If the nasal tip is droopy because the lateral cartilage is really long, then the lateral cartilage must be shortened or the tip won’t come up. That is why it is so important to find a rhinoplasty specialist to consult with so that he/she can analyze your nose and give you a proper opinion.
Will I see good results with rhinoplasty with my bulbous thick skin nose ?
Rhinoplasty with thick skin…
Dec 21, 2020
Just because you have thick skin does not mean you can’t get a nice improvement with rhinoplasty. It’s true that someone with thinner skin may get a more dramatic result, but a well performed rhinoplasty should certainly make the tip look thinner. The recovery is not any more difficult, but it may take a longer to get your final, desired result. Be sure to see a rhinoplasty specialist for consultation.

What are the chances of having another callus develop after a revision rhinoplasty to correct it?
Reducing a callus…
Nov 14, 2020
Usually, the callus will not reform if just some filing takes place. The callus often forms due to reducing and infracturing the bones, which is often not needed when just taking down the callus. Be sure to see a rhinoplasty specialist for consultation.

Nasal obstruction and rhinoplasty…
Nov 14, 2020
A nasal obstruction can be a significant septal deviation and/or deformed nasal cartilage. It is very important to see a rhinoplasty expert that can examine you and determine exactly what has to be done. Usually it’s a surgical procedure that can straighten the septum and refine the tip cartilage to both give you a nice shape and a great breathing passage.
