Am I a candidate for a tip rhinoplasty?
Options for 3/4 view nasal concavity
Sep 18, 2017
I have performed rhinoplasties on several patients with this issue, and it’s not necessary to do a rhinoplasty that includes osteotomies if you are otherwise satisfied with your nose. A cartilage onlay and or spreader graft is necessary to correct the concavity of your right upper lateral cartilage. A temporary solution would be to have filler expertly placed there; this usually lasts 6 – 12 months.
What do I need, a rhinoplasty or tip plasty?
Aesthetic harmony in rhinoplasty
Sep 18, 2017
While I agree the main issue with your nose is the boxy tip and asymmetric nostrils, I would recommend a rhinoplasty rather than just a tip plasty. The nose will look best (and more importantly look less distracting) when the entire nose is addressed. Therefore you will need osteotomies to narrow the nasal bones and middle portion of your nose to go along with your new narrow, more triangular tip.
