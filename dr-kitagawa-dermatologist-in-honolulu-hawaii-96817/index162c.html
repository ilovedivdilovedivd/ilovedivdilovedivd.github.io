Pink ink turning brown after laser removal. Is this normal?
Pink Ink Turning Brown With Laser? Light Inks Oxidize Before Laser Removal
May 23, 2018
It’s perfectly normal for a light-colored, white-based tattoo ink — such as pink — to darken before fading. The laser energy oxidizes the white pigment, turning it a brownish color. The laser then targets the brown and removes that, too. This double-step process is why removing light colors takes longer than removing dark colors. A board-certified dermatologist who’s an expert with PicoWay laser tattoo removal will be able to remove all traces of your butterfly.
Can you use less Botox to just weaken muscle instead of completely paralyzing it?
Don’t Paralyze Muscles With Botox? Botox Shouldn’t Paralyze; Keep It Natural
May 23, 2018
I don’t know of any instances in the cosmetic use of Botox where it would be advisable to actually paralyze a muscle. If you did, you’d get that “frozen” look that Botox was infamous for in the early days of the treatment. Instead, your dermatologist gives you just enough to relax the muscle so it doesn’t form wrinkles anymore. Your muscle can move, but can’t contract fully. A board-certified dermatologist will be able to help you get beautiful results from Botox.
