Gold Coast Nose Surgery

Rhinoplasty in Gold Coast is a section of plastic surgery dealing with the correction of congenital or acquired deformities of the nose, as well as the restoration of the nose.

With the help of different types of rhinoplasty is possible increase or decrease the size of the nose, change its shape, and the elimination of breathing problems.

Aesthetic aim is harmonization of facial features due to correction of various defects of the nose. Rhinoplasty is usually performed for persons older than 15-17 years.

The aim of rhinoplasty in Queensland is to restore the correct forms and functions of the nose, disturbed during embryogenesis, and also as a result of diseases and injuries (mechanical, thermal, and others.) obtained during the life.

Rhinoplasty includes the following types of surgical interventions: a septoplasty, the reposition the nasal bones, removal the nasal hump, shortening the nose, rapprochement of nasal bones, support the tip of the nose, the correction the tip of nose, full correction of nose.

Closed or open accesses can be used depending on the purposes of the nose job. Closed rhinoplasty is performed through an incision in the nasal cavity with a abruption of the skin of the nose framework (nasal bones and cartilage).

Through the closed access is carried out the surgery to change the shape of the nose, removal of excess bone or cartilage of the nose and so on. In the case of a large volume of interventions is used an open rhinoplasty with incisions to the columella – vertical skinfold separating the nostrils.

Queensland Minor Nose Job

Gold Coast Rhinoplasty can be primary or secondary. The secondary or revision rhinoplasty is performed in order to eliminate defects remaining after the primary rhinoplasty.

The patients resorting to this method, if necessary correction small drawbacks of the nose: rounding the tip of the nose, change the shape the nose wings, restoring symmetry and so on.

Planning and carrying out rhinoplasty

Planning begins with a consultation the plastic surgeon who carefully examines condition of the tissue and the patient’s nose shape, reveals the presence of disorders of nasal breathing function.

Procedure Of Rhinoplasty In Gold Coast Before And After Results

In order to achieve the best results are performed photographs and computer simulations of the nose. Consultation of the otolaryngologist is shown before performing rhinoplasty.

Rhinoplasty is performed jointly by a plastic surgeon and otolaryngologist with pronounced nose defects and violation of nasal breathing. Usually rhinoplasty is performed under general anesthesia combined with local infiltration anesthesia. Duration of nose reshaping is approximately 1-2 hours or more depending on the complexity.

During rhinoplasty in Gold Coast is performed a surgical change bones and cartilages of the nose in order to give the nose more favorable external size and shape (change of length, width and height, correction the nasal septum deviation, eliminating the hump, the nose tip correction, and so on.).

Postoperative scars are practically invisible in the case of well-executed rhinoplasty and lack of complications.

Postoperative recovery

Tampons are inserted into the nasal passages after rhinoplasty, which are removed after 2-3 days after nose reshape surgery. Special plaster cast is applied for 5-7 days (intervention on the cartilage of the nose) or 12-14 days (intervention on the bone of the nose) for fixing the new shape of the nose.

Pain is usually moderate, nasal breathing due to swelling in the nasal cavity may be difficult in the first few weeks. Bleeding and swelling in the nose and around the eyes disappear within 10-14 days.

Before And After Nose Reshaping In Gold Coast

During the 1.5 months after rhinoplasty is not recommended the wearing of glasses. A full course of recovery after rhinoplasty is up to 6-8 months, so the final results are evaluated after six months or a year after nose beauty surgery.

Medical risks and complications of rhinoplasty

As in any other surgical interventions there is a possibility of damage of blood vessels and septic complications. Primary rhinoplasty may be required in case incomplete correction of existing defects.

Secondary rhinoplasty is the most complex intervention and can be performed no earlier than 6 months after the initial nose job. Conducting secondary rhinoplasty may be required for the wrong stitched cartilages, excessive removal of cartilage, external nose asymmetry and in some other cases.
