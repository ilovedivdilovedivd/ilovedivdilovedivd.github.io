What do you think would improve my nose? It is broken, needs a cartilage graft and asymmetrical
rhinoplasty for asymmetry of the nose
Jan 30, 2020
It appears from the photos that your nose is deviated and is asymmetric.  To address these issues, both the cartilage as well as the bones of the nose need attention.  You would benefit from a septorhinoplasty to help correct the deviation of the nose and restore symmetry to the nose.  These are involved procedure and require a surgeon with a lot of experience in crooked noses and rhinoplasty.  
Do I have a deviated septum or is my nose just crooked? What could I do to fix this issue? (Pho
Crooked nose versus deviated septum
Jan 30, 2020
A nose will appear crooked for multiple reasons one of which can be a deviated septum.  A crooked nose itself does not necessarily mean your septum is deviated.  However, if the septum is deviated it has to be addressed to correct a deviated nose.  Rhinoplasty surgeons always need to address a deviated septum at the time of surgery to ensure both proper function and aesthetic of the nose.    
Can an osteotomy give at least 1-1.5mm height to a nose bridge that is not deficient in height?
raising the nasal bridge height
Jan 6, 2020
Osteotomies do not provide height to the nasal bridge.  They primarily serve to narrow the width of the nasal bones but do not provide for additional nasal bridge height.  Augmentation of the bridge is achieved is achieved separately via other rhinoplasty techniques.   
How much millimeters can surgery project the nose?
Rhinoplasty limitations
Nov 25, 2019
The degree of projection of the nose as in other aspects of rhinoplasty has its limitations based on the anatomy the of the nose.  Factors such as the degree of skin thickness will limit the projection of the nose.  Every case has to be individualized but in general the most projection can be achieved in patients with pre-existing tip cartilage under-projection and thicker skin.  In such cases these maneuvers help to create more definition to the tip.  
What kind of nose job would work best for me?
Goals for rhinoplasty
Nov 1, 2019
Based on your photos and goals, you appear to be a very good candidate for rhinoplasty.  Rhinoplasty can lower the bridge of the nose and elevate and support the tip of the nose from the profile.  From the front, the bridge can be narrowed and the tip refined.  It is best to choose a surgeon you have met and you feel comfortable they can attain your goals.  Discuss with them your concerns about the anesthesia and your options for anesthesia.
