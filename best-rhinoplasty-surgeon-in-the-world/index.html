Qualified rhinoplasty surgeon

In theory rhinoplasty can be thought of as purely cosmetic (addressing aesthetic concerns only), purely functional (addressing breathing concerns only) or a combination of both.

The reality is that the two cannot be entirely separated – changing the appearance of the nose can result in impaired breathing and functional surgery to improve the nasal airway can have a negative impact on appearance.

So it’s very important to find a surgeon that is highly competent at both to ensure that your aesthetic goals are met and that your nasal airway function is maintained or substantially improved if necessary.When seeking rhinoplasty information some important points for consideration include:

  1. Is the surgeon board certified or double board certified? Surgeons that are double board certified more often have extensive experience that includes cosmetic rhinoplasty, surgical management of the nasal airway and revision rhinoplasty.
  2. Who do doctors in the community send the more difficult rhinoplasty cases to? (J. David Holcomb, MD, Sarasota Facial Plastic Surgeon)

Advice to Find the Best Rhinoplasty Surgeon

Rhinoplasty is a complex and difficult operation to master. It has also evolved through the years to focus not only on form, but function. In the past, many people who were operated on by skilled surgeons came away with nice looking noses that were difficult to breathe through.

Modern rhinoplasty aims to improve the aesthetics of the nose without sacrificing the patient’s ability to comfortably breathe. It is also geared towards lasting results. The factors you should look for to help identify someone who possesses these abilities are as follows:

  • Board Certification – Chose someone who has board certification in Facial Plastic Surgery or Plastic SurgeryTraining – Where did they train? Big programs often have a wider breadth of experience.
  • Photos – Look at both general examples of their before and after photos, but also try to see if you can find an example of a nose that had a similar appearance to yours.
  • Computer Simulations – This can be an educational experience for both you and the surgeon. You can learn what the surgeon has in mind (just remember that anything is possible on a computer simulation, but not everything is achievable in the OR) and the surgeon learns what your desires. This will ensure that the two of you are on the same page.
  • Personality – This is a team effort. You should feel very comfortable with the surgeon and his staff. You are building a relationship and will likely see him/her many times in the year after the procedure, so you want to feel very confident in your choice. Don’t have an elective operation with a surgeon who you do not have a good connection with. (Sirius K. Yoo, MD, San Diego Facial Plastic Surgeon)

Find a surgeon who listens to your desire and how you want your nose to look. Extensive experience and knowledge count the most, (Vartan Mardirossian, MD, Jupiter Facial Plastic Surgeon)

Rhinoplasty is an extraordinary surgery. Essentially a rhinoplasty is really a long surgery made up of individual surgical maneuvers, each with it’s own goal for execution and outcome. Your surgeon plans your outcome by combining a series of mini-procedures appropriate for your goals.

It is important for the surgeon to anticipate how changes in one area affect others both during your surgery and also during healing. Remember that a rhinoplasty will heal for at least a year, and more realistically a few years after your procedure is completed.

The best surgeons for any procedure will combine training, experience, artistry, and attention to detail with communication and personality that makes the experience exceptional for the patient.

Ask about the surgeon’s experience, speak with past patients, and seek a few consultations before making a choosing the right surgeon for you. (Todd Christopher Hobgood, MD, Phoenix Facial Plastic Surgeon)

Finding the right doctor for Rhinoplasty takes some work…

Rhinoplasty is complex surgery so it is important to take steps to find the right surgeon. The most important criteria is the experience of the surgeon, and this is broken down into two parts. First, it is best if the surgeon has several years of experience.

Their specific training is not as important as how long they have been performing rhinoplasty, because little details are learned on every case that is done. These details add up over time to being able to provide excellent, consistent results.

Second, your surgeon should be performing rhinoplasty on a regular basis. Some plastic surgeons have a lot of experience, but only perform a few rhinoplasties every year. It is best to find a surgeon who performs rhinoplasty every week. Once you are in for a consultation, look through the albums, and you should be able to see MANY rhinoplasty photos, not just a handful. This will ensure that he or she performs rhinoplasty regularly.

Ask the surgeon which hospital he or she is on staff with. This is something that you can check, and the hospitals do extensive background checks on their doctors so you don’t have to. Finally, determine if you are getting a good feeling about the surgeon; whether he or she really listens to you, and you feel like the surgeon is someone you can trust. If your gut is telling you something else you should listen to it. (Andrew Miller, MD, Edison Facial Plastic Surgeon)
