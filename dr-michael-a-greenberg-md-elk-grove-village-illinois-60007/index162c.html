What is the Recovery Time for Voluma in Upper Cheeks?
Voluma Healing Time
May 10, 2019
I’ll answer this from personal experience, as I’ve had injections myself.   I did not notice much swelling, but I had a small bruise that went away quickly.  I was somewhat sore to the touch for only two days or so.  I find the same experience in most of my patients.   I think the response is individual and also the result of injection techniques.
How long does Juvederm & Voluma really last?
How Long Does Voluma Last?
May 10, 2019
I love using Voluma because of the great results, and in my experience, minimal swelling and bruising.  How long it lasts is a sum of many factors.  I find that individuals “burn it” at various speeds, but for most of my patients, a year and a half to two is the usual time.  
Is it okay that cheek is tender to touch 4 weeks after Voluma?
Four Weeks Later
May 10, 2019
I would return to my doctor and have them check the area.  If you only feel discomfort with pressure, it’s probably okay. Some trauma takes that long or longer to heal.
If you apply Aloe Vera 3 hours a day, will it lighten the flat mole on your face?
Aloe Vera
May 10, 2019
Aloe is great stuff and perfect for a sunburn.  That’s why every hotel in Aruba grows it — so you can just pick a leaf and use it.  I do not believe it has any lightening qualities.   I suggest you see a dermatologist and have your mole evaluated.  If you don’t like it, there are ways to remove it with minimal scarring.
Swollen lip, lumps or doctor mistake?
Too Early To Judge
May 10, 2019
It’s way too early to judge the results you are going to get from your filler.  There is always swelling and sometimes bruising in lip fillers.  I suggest that you wait about a week or so and then re-evaluate.  And don’t look in the mirror every ten minutes!  Healing takes its time even in the days of instant everything.
How long does bruising and swelling last after using Voluma?
Bruising with Voluma
May 7, 2019
In my practice, I see very little bruising with Voluma even though it is reported as a common side effect.  However, yours should last a few weeks.  The swelling will also decrease.  I think the answer for you is to call and see the physician who injected you for support and treatment.
