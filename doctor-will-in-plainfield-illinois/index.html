Non-surgical Nose Job Procedures to Reduce Nose Size?
Nonsurgical Rhinoplasty
Jul 6, 2017
Injectables (such as Restylane and Juvederm) are used as tools in nonsurgical rhinoplasty. They are useful in masking a hump on the bridge of the nose or improving asymmetries of the nose. Though it is not reducing the size of the nose, it can make the nose “appear smaller” by making it look more proportional. It is important to consult with a rhinoplasty surgeon to ensure that you understand all your options.
Is It Possible That With Age, My Nose Is Growing Larger?
Aging of the Nose
Jul 6, 2017
With aging, can come changes with the nose. Laxity or weakening of the cartilages and support system of the nose can make the nose appear longer. Also, changes in the skin type (larger pores, increased oil production, acne, rosacea), can alter the appearance of the nasal tip making it seem larger and more bulbous. Rhinoplasty may be a great option to restore the nose back to its original shape.

What are the signs and symptoms of a broken nose?
Nasal Fracture
Mar 30, 2017
After a blunt injury to the nose, patients will have an episode of bleeding. This in of itself does not mean that the bones are broken. A nasal fracture (broken nose) is associated difficulty breathing for days after the injury, crookedness of nose (remaining after swelling resolved), or severe pain when touching the nasal bones. A good physical examination and possible X-rays will help determine if a fracture occurred and if any intervention is necessary.

Any bruising is usually resolved after one week. Patients can start to wear make-up at this time as well. Exercise is allowed after 2 weeks.

How Much Does Rhinoplasty Cost?
Cost of Rhinoplasty
Mar 30, 2017
A rhinoplasty procedure can cost from $4000-$10,000 depending on the recommended procedure, where it is performed, and physician.
