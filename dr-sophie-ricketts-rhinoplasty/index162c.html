Is a rhinoplasty suitable for my nose shape?
Good candidate for rhinoplasty
Jul 28, 2019
You would be a good candidate for an open rhinoplasty to address the hump, tip projection/shape/position. All noses are challenging surgeries because it is so precise. You have not had previous surgery which is great, and the things that are to be changed about your nose are very common amongst rhinoplasty patients. Take a look at my profile and weblink for a few examples, if that helps.
Would straightening my nose help?
Straightening a deviated septum
Jul 25, 2019
You have a nose that deviates to your left. This is a combination of deviation of the nasal bones as well as the septum and a rhinoplasty would certainly create more symmetry for your face. There are many people that have facial asymmetry in the absence of trauma, so there may not be a reason!
To straighten it would involve a rhinoplasty including osteotomies to move the nasal bones and a septoplasty to centralise the septum.
