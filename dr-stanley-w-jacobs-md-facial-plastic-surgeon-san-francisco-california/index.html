What’s wrong with my nose? Do I have a deviated septum?
uneven nostrils
May 24, 2015
Yes, you have a deviated septum. It is the lower or caudal end of your septum that needs correcting, by a septoplasty. The septum has shifted off your maxillary crest and need repositioning. It is not complicated. Once that is done your nostrils will be more even.
My nose has a spongy, bulbous tip. Can rhinoplasty correct this to achieve a more structured look
Bulbous nasal tip
May 24, 2015
To improve a bulbous nasal tip, you need to have lateral crural extension strut cartilage grafts to reinforce your floppy lower lateral cartilages. These are septal cartilage grafts. The tip cartilages also need sewing together at the dome to get better tip definition. And if your subcutaneous tissue can be thinned , that would also help.

How can I make my nose thinner?
Thinner nose
