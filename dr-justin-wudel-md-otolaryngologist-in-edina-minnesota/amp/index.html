Kybella dissolves the fat in your chin. Will it ever be available for other areas that have excess fat
Kybella for other areas?
Jan 13, 2017
Kybella is FDA approved for the removal of submental fat, and while it is tempting to think about injecting Kybella into other areas, I think it is best served in areas requiring smaller volumes of fat removal. The amount of Kybella needed to remove fat in the posterior arms, flanks, or abdomen would make the product cost prohibitive for most patients and one could get a faster and more affordable result with liposuction.

12 weeks post open-rhinoplasty will my tip size decrease?
Tip size decrease?
Jan 2, 2017
It takes a long time for nasal swelling to go out of the nose following an open rhinoplasty procedure. We tell patients to expect a full year before the swelling is completely gone. At your next appointment I would discuss this with your surgeon, as sometimes an injection of steroid can help with scar tissue and any residual swelling that is lingering. Best of luck!

How to get rid of a scar on lip from stitch?
Scar from stitch
Dec 2, 2016
Hey there,

It is difficult to tell from the picture whether or not that lip scar is actually a vascular lesion, scarring, or what we sometimes call traumatic tattooing. This can happen if debris is trapped under the skin when the injury occurred. My suggestion is to have the lesion excised and re-closed. The vertical lines in the lips camouflage scars very nicely in this area and I think this will provide you with an excellent result!

Best laser for under eye fine lines and creepiness?
Laser for under eye fine lines
Nov 8, 2016
Hi there -

I would recommend either Halo laser or Profractional Erbium Yag laser. The nice part about Halo is that it comes with less downtime than traditional fractionated lasers. You might need more than one treatment to get your desired results, but this can be true for other laser types as well. Furthermore, the collagen stimulation you get with Halo to improve the fine lines and wrinkles will continue to be produced up to 3 months after your treatment.
