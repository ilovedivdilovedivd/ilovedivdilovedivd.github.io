Wide nostrils can be fixed

Yes, wide nostrils can be fixed. The procedure involves reducing the outward curve of the nostril . The nostril opening itself can be made smaller if needed.

You must keep the nose in balance with your other facial features so you may need a complete rhinoplasty. Have consultation with a plastic surgeon to discuss your desires. (Sheila Bond, MD, Montclair Plastic Surgeon)

WIde nostrils certainly can be made narrower if they are definitely wide. This is a fairly simple procedure. (Steven Wallach, MD, Manhattan Plastic Surgeon)

Wide nostrils repaired through rhinoplasty

Wide or flared nostrils is a common complaint in nasal shaping, and it is very common to see the base of the nose widen after surgery on the upper jaw. Rhinoplasty can reduce the width and flare and reshape the nostril and bring your old nose back, and more if you wish. Best of luck, peterejohnsonmd (Peter E. Johnson, MD, Chicago Plastic Surgeon)

Reducing a Wide Nasal Base

Any type of facial surgery where the jaw is moved can result in changes relating to the appearance of the nose – especially in making it look wider.

If this occurs after surgery or naturally, there are surgical techniques called an alar base resection which is performed by removing wedges of tissues at the base of the nose and the scar sits in the crease where the nose meets the cheek.

Narrowing your Nostrils

In most cases, a small wedge of tissue can be excised (removed) from the nostril. This will help narrow the nose and soften the angle between your nostril and your face giving a smoother transition. (David Shafer, MD, New York Plastic Surgeon)
