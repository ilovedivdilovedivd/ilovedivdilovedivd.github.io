You must do your Homework to choose the best nose surgeon

Your question about how to pick a nose surgeon is an extremely important one. As you already alluded to it’s a personal decision and one that you may not want to share with friends as far as who to choose or use. That being said it requires a lot of research and there multiple facets to examine when making this decision.

Best nose surgeon photo

The first point to be addressed is the training and certification of the surgeon. It is extremely important to choose someone who has extensive formalized training for this procedure.

Preferably someone who has been trained and is Board Certified in Ear, Nose, and Throat (ENT) surgery, and also has subsequent facial plastic surgery training is ideal. The nose not only is a structure of beauty but also one of function, and the worst thing to do is have a rhinoplasty that turns out looking good but you can’t breathe well through as a result.

Nothing is guaranteed with this procedure but choosing someone who understands not only its aesthetic appearance but also its function is extremely important, and having grounded formalized years of training to understand the basics of this is extremely important.

Best nose surgeon picture

The second point to consider is someone who had plastic surgeon training with a high volume of rhinoplasties in the program. Plastic Surgery training varies widely on its focus (i.e. face, body, burns, craniofacial/pediatric, etc…) depending on where the surgeon trained, and who they trained under.

Some training programs are much more heavily weighted towards reconstruction or perhaps surgery on structures other than the nose, so it’s important to inquire into that as well when initially meeting your surgeon, or researching him or her ahead of time.

The third point to consider is volume of surgery since their training, i.e.: their personal experience. Rhinoplasty is a very humbling procedure even for surgeons with years of experience and high volume/year. It truly is a procedure that continues to teach the surgeon nuances throughout their career.

Best nose surgeon result

Noses have different levels of challenges, or another way to look at it is no two noses are alike as you can see just from looking at those around you. What the translation of this is that since no two noses are alike, no two rhinoplasties are alike either, and therefore a surgeon gains judgment and skill from EVERY nose he operates on.

Over the years this yields a better understanding of the nuances of rhinoplasty. Some of the simpler noses can be done by someone who doesn’t have much experience, but the majority of them require much more knowledge and skill gained from a healthy volume not only on a yearly basis, but also over many years.

The next point would be to see rhinoplasty results of that surgeon. It is important to note that those post-operative photographs are preferentially a year out since swelling can hide subtle nuances that aren’t visible during the first couple months post operatively.

The best nose surgeon

You should expect to see many different rhinoplasty results which will reflect the surgeon having significant breadth of experience, since it is rare for most patients to give permission to use their photos, since pictures of the nose definitely identify the patient by their face. Another important point to consider is where the surgery will be performed.

Nose surgery should be performed in an accredited facility and preferentially performed under general anesthesia so that the airway is protected. Otherwise, during the procedure there can be the potential for some blood to trickle down the back of the throat and can cause problems with the patient’s airway. If the airway isn’t protected by the use of an endotracheal tube, the blood in the back of the throat/on the vocal cords can result in the patient moving or coughing during this delicate procedure.

Best nose surgeon before and after

Therefore, consider not only the accreditation of the facility but what type of anesthesia will be used by that surgeon. Last and equally important is your trust with the surgeon. If you search on the Internet about rhinoplasty you will learn that there is also something called revision rhinoplasty.

Any surgeon worth his wait that does significant volumes of rhinoplasty will have to “touch up” (also known as revision rhinoplasty) of some of their own patients. The rate varies widely as far as what a revision rate is for a surgeon depending on again their volume and years of experience as well as their skill and judgment. When doing a consultation it is reasonable to ask what the surgeons revision rate is. If the surgeon tells you zero you should run, not walk, because that is not being honest and accurate for anybody who performs this procedure, and aspires to obtain an excellent result for their patients.

A surgeon who has a revision rate below 5 percent with a reasonable volume of yearly rhinoplasty would be a great place to start. It’s important to understand how a surgeon handles a revision financially, should you require a revision of his surgery. This should be an honest and open discussion and goes back to the beginning of this point in that you should have full trust in the physician that will be performing your surgery. Should the situation arise that there is something that needs to be touched up, you need to feel comfortable bringing it up as well as knowing ahead of time the surgeon’s protocol in dealing with this. (Burke Robinson, MD, Atlanta Facial Plastic Surgeon)

Choosing a Rhinoplasty Surgeon

Rhinoplasty surgery is considered to be one of the most difficult procedures performed by plastic surgeons. The procedure requires that the surgeon have multiple skill sets. He or she must be an anatomist, physiologist, psychologist, architect, and engineer.

Most importantly, the procedure requires that the surgeon be an artist. Many factors must be considered when choosing a rhinoplasty surgeon. These include the surgeon’s experience, reputation, certification, training, and ability to communicate with patients.

When performing rhinoplasty surgery, the analysis of the problem is critical. The surgeon must look at the nose from multiple perspectives. The nose should be evaluated based on its individual characteristics as well as its relationship with surrounding facial structures. It’s important to achieve facial harmony, balance, and proportion to obtain optimal aesthetic results.

It’s also important to consider how nasal surgery will interact with the patient’s gender, ethnic background, and nasal physiology. Finally, every patient has unique aesthetic goals and it’s important that these be understood by the surgeon as well. Although this type of analysis is important, the ability of the surgeon to communicate with patients is even more important. For these reasons, several factors should be considered when choosing a rhinoplasty surgeon. These include:

  1. The ability to evaluate and form a treatment plan for your nose
  2. The ability to communicate the game plan so that it’s understandable
  3. The use of computer imaging to help you visualize the proposed endpoint
  4. Before and after pictures to demonstrate prior work
  5. Board Certification
  6. Training
  7. Experience
  8. Reputation Careful evaluation of these parameters should point you in the right direction. Don’t hesitate to get multiple opinions and most importantly, trust your instincts. (Richard J. Bruneteau, MD, Omaha Plastic Surgeon)
