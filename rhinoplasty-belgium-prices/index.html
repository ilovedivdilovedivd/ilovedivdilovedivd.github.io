Rhinoplasty Belgium Cost

Rhinoplasty is one of the most popular plastic surgery in the world. This operation is directed at correcting the form of the nose.

Usually rhinoplasty is recommended at age 14-15 years and over, that is when there is a full and final formation of the cartilages of the nose.

The age limit for rhinoplasty does not exist, main thing is that it allowed health of the patient. In some cases, rhinoplasty can be combined with a facelift or other procedures in order to give the face a youthful appearance.

Rhinoplasty In Belgium is also sometimes combined with other interventions on the nose when there is difficulty in nasal breathing as a result of, for example, deformation of the nasal septum, the presence of nasal turbinates, and so on.

It is necessary to consult with your doctor and determine the indications for such intervention.

Indications for rhinoplasty

Who is a good candidate for a rhinoplasty? Usually, people who have complex about the shape of its nose resort to rhinoplasty. For example, you do not like the small hump, or you feel that your nose is too long.

Or, on the contrary, do you think that you have too short and ugly nose. In addition, rhinoplasty is used for restoration the nose shape after injuries, burns, or abnormalities of the nose.

Moreover, as already mentioned above, rhinoplasty can be combined with other interventions to restore the nasal cavity for nasal breathing, in case of indications.

Surgery On The Nose In Ghent Belgium Before And After Photos

Rhinoplasty in Belgium allows to make the correct shape of the nose, harmonious with the rest of the face, in the correct proportions, make it smooth, reduce, or vice versa, to give it volume and to form the beautiful tip of the nose.

The main indications for rhinoplasty are:

  1. Too big nose
  2. hump on the nose, which is not pleasant to the patient
  3. too wide nose
  4. an ugly tip of the nose, in the form of hanging down drops
  5. thick or wide nose tip
  6. too wide nostrils
  7. a hook-shaped nose
  8. injury or deformation of the nose.

It is important to understand what you want from the operation, and at the same time aware that there are some limitations, as the plastic surgeon is not a sculptor, and the patient’s nose is not a stone or granite, from which simply can be cut off all superfluous.

Furthermore, it is important to understand that the process of healing and postoperative course depends on many factors. Ideally, the patient must understand that rhinoplasty can not make of him another human.
