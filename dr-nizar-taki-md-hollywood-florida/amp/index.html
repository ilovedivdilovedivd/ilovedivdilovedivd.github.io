A transcutaneous approach will indeed allow the removal of both extra skin and eyebags. A transconjunctival approach by itself would not allow the removal of extra skin, but a "skin pinch" can be performed afterwards to remove the extra skin. The skin pinch has less risk of ectropion since it is a removal of only skin and does not traverse the structures deep to that. You should consult with a board certified surgeon experienced in these procedures and let him or her choose the technique...
