Rhinoplasty and chin implant combination

The rhinoplasty and chin implant combination is very common in our practice and helps significantly with facial balance.

The photograph demonstrates an overly projecting nose, and an under projecting chin. The rhinoplasty procedure would involve shaving the dorsal hump down and decreasing projection of the nose, while a chin implant would be inserted through a sub-mental approach to give added projection to the chin.

Chin implants are manufactured in a variety of sizes and shapes which is best determined at the time of the consultation. For many examples of chin implant and rhinoplasty performed together and our price list, please see the link below (William Portuese, MD, Seattle Facial Plastic Surgeon)

If you want to have improvement in your nose and chin profile, then the procedures you mention may be worthwhile for you. (Steven Wallach, MD, Manhattan Plastic Surgeon)

Chin implant with a rhinoplasty

Reduction of the nose to get it in balance with the rest of the face is an important goal when planning a rhinoplasty. A chin implant will help significantly to achieve improved balance for the whole face.

At the same time a very minimal liposuction (perhaps only 5-10cc) may also may also help separate your jawline from the neck. If you were to have some suctioning in that area, that fat would no longer be there to increase in size, if you gain some weight.

When I do the chin implant along with a rhinoplasty there is usually not too much additional expense for the extra time and the implants are not too expensive. I believe that you may be a good candidate for this combination of procedures.  (John M. Weeter, MD, Louisville Plastic Surgeon)
