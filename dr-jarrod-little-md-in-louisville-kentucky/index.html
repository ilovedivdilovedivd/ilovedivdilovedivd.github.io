Rhinoplasty/Nose Job: Why do most surgeons avoid deprojecting the nose?
Change in nasal projection
Aug 6, 2020
Changing the projection of the nose is not the difficult part. The difficulty lies in obtaining stable, long-term results. In order to de-project the nose, cartilage must be removed or repositioned. These maneuvers can destabilize the nasal tip support and lead to more deprojection than anticipated in the long-term. As always, it is important to consult with a board-certified Plastic Surgeon who specializes in Rhinoplasty.

Narrowing the nostril width is surprisingly complicated for obtaining stable, long-term results. Nevertheless, narrowing your nostril width is attainable. As always, it is important that you consult with a board-certified Plastic Surgeon who specializes in Rhinoplasty.
