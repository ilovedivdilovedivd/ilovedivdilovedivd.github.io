Michigan Nose Job Cost

Rhinoplasty in Michigan is one of the types of plastic surgery aimed at correcting congenital and acquired defects of the nose structure. Rhinoplasty is a leader in aesthetic surgery. However, in reality, the correction is needed not only to improve the appearance, but also to restore health.

Description of the procedure

The future “new” form of a nose or its tip is modeled by a special and quite a sophisticated computer program in conjunction with a physician.

Then, experts agree upon the course of plastic surgery with all the numerous details. Today there are many options of rhinoplasty. In general, the nose job is a detaching soft tissue and outcrop bones and cartilages, changing their shape and size, and the subsequent suturing.

The surgical access may be direct or lateral, and rhinoplasty open and closed. How much does cost each of them is not as important as the final result.

Of course, the price of more modern methods is higher than the cost of interventions by the classical scheme.

Indications for rhinoplasty

– Damage and the curvature of the nose as a result of injuries;
– Congenital deformation, affecting the nasal breathing;
– The curvature of the nasal septum (septoplasty);
– Pointed, thickened or hooked tip of the nose;
– Saddle-shaped nose and a “snub” nose;
– Excessive length from the nose bridge to the tip;
– Extended or too narrowed nostrils.

Contraindications

One of the major contraindications to this plastic surgery is age. The final formation of bone and cartilage tissue is derived from 20 to 25 years.

However, in case of injuries at a younger age, as well as congenital anomalies, impeding normal breathing through the nose, rhinoplasty is necessary.

Other contraindications include:
  1. Chronic diseases of the cardiovascular system, liver and kidneys;

  2. Diabetes mellitus, abnormalities of blood coagulation;
  3. Purulent and allergic rashes and acne in the correction area;
  4. Acute bacterial and viral infections, and chronic, that causing a severe depletion of the body (eg, tuberculosis);
  5. Neoplasms (both malignant and benign).

This group contraindications is characterized for the entire surgery in general. However, in most cases, rhinoplasty can be performed some time after complete recovery.

The process carrying out of procedure

At first you are discussing purpose of plastic surgery and choose the optimal form via a special program together with an experienced surgeon of our clinic.

The patient is in the operating room after preparation. Usually rhinoplasty is performed under local anesthesia, and only in rare cases, under general anesthesia.

Finally, a qualified doctor performs all the necessary manipulations for correction. After nose reshape surgery incisions are closed with stitches, and superimposed a fixing tire and sterile bandages.

  • swelling of the tissues of the nose goes away a few days later; During this period it is recommended to breathe only through the mouth;
