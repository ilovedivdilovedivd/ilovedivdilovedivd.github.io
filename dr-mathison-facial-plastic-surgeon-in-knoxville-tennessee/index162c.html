Can my eyebrows be fixed after Botox?
Forehead Botox
Apr 5, 2017
Based on your pictures, it appears that your Botox injections were placed mainly in the center portion of the brow.  This can lead to a lowering of your inner eyebrows and a resulting “evil look.”  Additional Botox in the outer aspects of the brow can help balance things out.  You can also wait until the Botox wears off, which usually takes approximately 3-4 months.Best of luck!
What is the recommended saline spray to use post-op?
Nasal Saline Spray
Mar 31, 2017
Most Over-The-Counter brands will suffice.  They will all contain some type of preservative, but these have been shown to cause no issues after surgery.  I recommend continuing your saline nasal spray for several weeks after surgery.  When in doubt, ask your surgeon.  They may want you to use it much, much longer.Best of luck!
Is it still safe and feasible to have plastic surgeries done if I am a carrier of staph?
Staph infection and cosmetic surgery
Mar 31, 2017
Staph is a relatively common skin bacteria.  However, recurring staph infections are nothing to gloss over.  The Mommy Makeover is what concerns me.  The blood supply to those surgical areas is not as robust as it is in the nose.  As such, the body has a harder time fighting off infection.  Your surgeon definitely needs to know about this issue.  He/She may want you to see an infectious disease specialist or, at a minimum, change your intra-op and post-op antibiotics to ones that covers staph.
Is neck-lift needed automatically when doing facelift?
Face and Neck Surgery
Mar 29, 2017
When one performs a facelift, it almost always involves some form of neck work.  Usually the muscles are tightened and/or excess skin is removed.  Based on your pictures, you don’t need much work on your neck.  However, I do agree with your cosmetic surgeon in that you will probably need neck work done in the future, and at that time a revision facelift will be necessary.Best of luck!
