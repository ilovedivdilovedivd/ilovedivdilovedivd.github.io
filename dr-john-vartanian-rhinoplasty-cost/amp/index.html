Can I have Buccal fat removal a month after Rhinoplasty?
Buccal fat removal
Aug 12, 2019
Technically you could have the buccal fat removal a month after rhinoplasty.  Having said that, I would recommend waiting 2-3 months so that any rhinoplasty related facial swelling settles down and your surgeon can use better judgement on how much buccal fat to remove.  I perform a large numbers of buccal fat removals under local anesthesia and patients tolerate it very comfortably.   All the best to you, @drjohnvartanian

What Kind of Rhinoplasty Would Be Needed for my Wide Nose and Bulbous Tip?
Refining bulbous tip – narrowing nose
Aug 25, 2011
Looking at the pictures one has to balance your desire for more tip refinement, with the real possibility of nasal obstruction and compromise. You see, to a certain extent when you narrow the tip, you are also narrowing the inside of the nose which could cause serious breathing problems. So think about that carefully and request a conservative degree of change from your surgeon to lessen that risk. Also, you may need spreader grafts to prevent nasal obstruction…
Where does the cartilage come from for a rhinoplasty revision for a pinched tip?
Revision Rhinoplasty Cartilage Sources
Aug 24, 2011
In some revision rhinoplasty cases cartilage needs to be added to the nose for support and/or looks. Correcting a pinched tip may be one such example. We generally get extra cartilage from the following places (in order of ease and general preference): Septum, ears, patient's own rib cartilage, and cadaveric rib cartilage. Hope this help out and all the best, Dr. Vartanian

Hi,  6-months after rhinoplasty your nose should be stable enough not to be harmed by extractions or a facial.
