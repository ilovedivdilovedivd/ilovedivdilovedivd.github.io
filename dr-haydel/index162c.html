Laser Hair Removal

Over time you will start to grow hair in the area you had treated. It may take a few years to get back to where it was before the treatments. I find males have their hair grow back faster than females.
Can Halo Laser make your skin look worse?
2 Weeks post Halo
Jul 30, 2018
Due to inflammation after any laser treatment there are times the pores/scars may appear worse. However it may also be to over use of product post treatment. We always recommend our patients to call our office with any concerns and to make an appt. to be checked if needed. Typical results will be seen in 4-8 weeks post Halo or any fractionated laser treatment. Make sure you aren't over using products and gently cleanse the skin. Give it time to heal and you will have a great halo glow!
What is the best treatment for hyperpigmentation after halo laser?
Treating PIH after Halo
Jun 11, 2018
The best thing to do would be to consult the office you had the Halo treatment done with to let them know how your skin is healing and with any questions regarding treatment. Depending on how long it has been after Halo you can start using hydroquinone creams to help lighten the hyperpigmentation or otherwise known as post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation Many times caused from the heat from laser treatments. Good news is that its not permanent and can be fixed. Hope this helps!

Can flat moles be removed at home with remedies without leaving a scar?
Mole removal
Jan 29, 2018
It is best not to treat real moles or nevi at home. A trained professional needs to help decide if the lesion is cancerous or not. Most moles are removed by shave removal and sent to the lab for pathology to prove if they are benign or not. Even if a mole is removed by a Dermatologist, it is not always permanent as some grow back over time.
Are red moles normal?
red moles
Jan 29, 2018
Red moles on the skin are usually a benign inherited lesion called an angioma. They have no cancer potential but they can have "irritation potential" if the bleed from trauma. It is made of a collection of blood cells so that is how they can bleed if scraped or rubbed to vigorously. They are not contagious and do not spred from scratching. They can be numbed and cauterized in the office very easily and heal well if a patient desires treatment.
