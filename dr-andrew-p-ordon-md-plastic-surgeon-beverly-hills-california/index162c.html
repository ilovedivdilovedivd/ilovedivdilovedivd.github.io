Fillers and rhinoplasty: For how long have surgeons been using fillers as a corrective tool?
Radiesse is best
Jul 24, 2015
About 10 years ago I noticed that the Radiesse package insert warned that placing it on bone or cartilage could cause it to ossify and become permanent. I realized this was ideal for correcting problems after rhinoplasty. I have since done a number of cases where injecting a filler in the nose saved the patient from undergoing revision surgery. HA fillers are not a bad idea for a temporary solution but if the patient likes the result radiesse should be used to provide a permanent fix.
Will I scar more because I don't have a lot of skin to remove?
Less skin removed is better.
Jun 1, 2015
Lot's of factors go into how you heal. Genetics, how the tissue is handled by the surgeon, the amount of tension on the closure and how much skin is excised. Skin thickness changes over distance, and suturing thick skin to thin skin is a sure way to get poor healing. The smaller the amount of skin excised, the more similar skin thickness is on both sides of the wound. So don't let it stop you, if you only have a little.
