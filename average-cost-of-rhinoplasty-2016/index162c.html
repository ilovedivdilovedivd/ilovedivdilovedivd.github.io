Average Cost Of Nose Job In United States

The price of any procedure is a result of the fee for the actual surgery as well as the costs related to having anesthesia and an operating room. Some surgeons have their own operating rooms and can control the cost of the entire procedure.

Others use surgery centers and hospitals, and there is a small chance of having to pay more after the procedure if more time is needed in the operating room than expected.

The average cost for a rhinoplasty varies widely depending on location and the extent of surgery, but a rough estimate is $3,000 to $20,000.

I caution that you should not choose your surgeon on price alone. Rhinoplasty is a delicate procedure, and revision surgery is very difficult (and much more expensive). (Dana Goldberg, MD, Jupiter Plastic Surgeon)

Rhinoplasty prices vary geographically, as well from surgeon to surgeon. I’ve seen fees around the U.S. for a new nose as low as $3000 in rural areas to more than $30,000 in major markets. In the greater Baltimore, all-inclusive nose job fees are very similar to those for new breasts – about $7000.

Revision rhinoplasty fees are more, and $9-10k is not uncommon. (Randolph Capone, MD, Baltimore Facial Plastic Surgeon)

Rhinoplasty Costs

The functional part of the surgery (septum, turbinates, and displaced nasal bones) may be covered by your health care insurance. The coverage may include the anesthesiologist and the facility with proper benefits.

Average cost of rhinoplasty operation

The cosmetic portion of the surgery (tip definition, nasal length, nasal dorsal bump) are not covered by your health care insurance. Because of multiple issues and potential insurance compensation for functional surgery, it is difficult to specify a fee however, if there is no insurance coverage the total average cost for the professional, anesthesia, and facility fees may range from $4000 – $6000.

Insurance coverage would reduce some of the cost. Consult a Board Certified Plastic Surgeon with rhinoplasty experience. (George C. Peck, Jr, MD, West Orange Plastic Surgeon)

Average cost of Rhinoplasty Surgery

Average rhinoplasty cost varies greatly across the United States and around the world. Rhinoplasty cost is always one of the first questions asked. However, rhinoplasty cost should be taken into consideration with many other factors when choosing your cosmetic nasal surgeon, such as training, board certification, patient reviews, before/after photographs, etc.

Rhinoplasty is not one surgery, but rather many potential procedures to improve both the function and appearance of the nose. The cost of rhinoplasty varies greatly, but it’s usually similar for plastic surgeons in your area.

For example, northern Virginia rhinoplasty may range from $2000 to $20,000. Nationwide average rhinoplasty cost is about $6000.

Average rhinoplasty cost is depends on several factors, but largely dependent on the following:

  • Region – New York rhinoplasty is more expensive than rural Midwest
  • Complexity – Full rhinoplasty treating many parts of the nose and requiring anesthesia is more expensive than simple procedures isolated to specific areas which may be performed with local anesthesia
  • Revision rhinoplasty – Revision surgery more expensive than primary surgery
  • Surgeon – Some plastic surgeons charge significantly more or less than their peers

In addition, portions of rhinoplasty surgery may also be covered by your insurance provider. The majority of cosmetic surgery is elective, so insurance plans don’t usually cover it.

However, there are exceptions and some plastic surgeons participate in the major insurance plans. Remember, plastic surgery covers a broad area of medicine which includes both reconstructive procedures and cosmetic procedures. If your procedure is medically indicated, some portion of the cost may be covered.

For example, Maryland patients with nasal allergy symptoms or sinus problems may combine their sinus surgery with rhinoplasty. (Houtan Chaboki, MD, Washington DC Facial Plastic Surgeon)

How much does rhinoplasty cost on average?

The cost of any surgical procedure will vary from one surgeon to another and rhinoplasty is no exception.

The cost will be based on surgeon fee, anaesthetic fee, and hospital fee, and these will all vary from surgeon to surgeon, as will the anaesthetic vary depending on the type of anaesthetic used and if it given by an anaesthetist, as well as if it is a general or local anaesthetic. The hospital fee, also know as the facility fee, will based on if the procedure is performed in office, or a day surgery, or a hospital with an overnight stay.

Before surgery all patients should have a cost estimate placed in front of them to help them decide which options and which surgeon they choose. This way patient know what to expect from the procedure as well as what all of the costs will be. My advice is a cost of procedure should include:

  1. Surgeon fee
  2. Anaesthetic fee
  3. Hospital fee/ facility fee

Expect the price to vary and be higher for

  1. More experienced/qualified surgeons
  2. General anaesthetic
  3. Acrredited hospital
  4. Overnight hospital stay

In short you get what you pay for, be sure that what yuo think is going to happen is going to happen, and be sure there are no added extra costs All the best Jeremy Hunt (Jeremy Hunt, MBBS, FRACS, Sydney Plastic Surgeon)

Cost of nose job

The fees for nose job vary depending on the complexity of the case. Nose job is the one of the most complex operations performed by plastic surgeons and with the increased complexity of the nose job comes the cost variation. A primary nose job will be less expensive than a revision nose job, and a septorhinoplasty to correct breathing difficulties will also be more costly. This in part is due to the increase in operating room time and subsequent increase OR and anesthesia fees.

The average surgeons fee for a primary nose job in the DC area is around $6500 to $8000, this does not include the operating room or anesthesia fees.

If you have a deviated septum with breathing difficulties your insurance may cover a portion of the procedure. Most importantly make sure your surgeons is board certified by the American Board of Plastic Surgery or the American Board of Otolaryngology and performs rhinoplastys frequently. (Rondi Kathleen Walker, MD, Washington Facial Plastic Surgeon)
