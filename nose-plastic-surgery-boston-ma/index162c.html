Nose Plastic Surgery In Boston, Massachusetts

There are several plastic surgery operations, which became the basis for the aesthetic correction of appearance. These include a rhinoplasty. The first mention of trying to change the nose shape dates back to ancient Rome.

Today rhinoplasty is a very popular around the world. It is proved that the operation on the nose can change the perception of the human appearance, giving it the attractiveness, proportionality and harmony of features.

The rhinoplasty operation

Rhinoplasty in Boston is a surgical intervention on the bone-cartilage framework that provides an aesthetic improvement of the appearance of the nose and restoration of nasal breathing.

Rhinoplasty involves shortening of the length of the nose, changes the width and depth of the nose, narrowing the nasal bridge, the position of the nasal tip, the size of the nostrils, a hump or depressions removal.

Today, rhinoplasty is classified into closed and open. Closed rhinoplasty is performed by cutting internal parts of the nostrils. Open rhinoplasty involves an additional small incision to the columella.

Method of rhinoplasty is negotiated with a plastic surgeon at the stage of preparatory procedures, depending on the volume of surgery.

Immediately after the surgery in the nasal passages of the patient is inserted tampons, and a special cast bandage is put on the nose to save the desired shape of the nose.

Nose job is performed using general anesthesia with the use of local anesthesia infiltration type. Rhinoplasty takes no more than one to two hours, however, some procedures may take longer.

Rhinoplasty after post-traumatic nose deformity

Nose job helps to eliminate post-traumatic deformation of the nose in case of ineffective reposition, or lack thereof.

Signs of ineffective repositioning:

  1. saddle nose;
  2. symmetry breaking in the areas of the tip and bridge of the nose;
  3. appearance hump;

  4. retraction of the cartilage in area of the nose;
  5. violation of nasal breathing (septum bending).

Rhinoplasty is appointed in 4-6 months from the time of injury, when fully disappear swelling and scar tissue will be formed. During the nose job, framework of the nose is cut, and its components will installed in the correct position, and then firmly fixed by a cast.

Nose job may require perform a septoplasty, in which there is an alignment of the nasal septum. If necessary, be allowed the use of bone and cartilage autotransplants.

Secondary rhinoplasty

If to the patient is needed a repeated rhinoplasty – Secondary rhinoplasty – between operations must elapse at least six months, and sometimes this period can last up to a year or more.

Boston, Massachusetts Rhinoplasty Before And After By Dr. Stuart H. Bentkover

If during the first surgery is removed a lot of bone, to recover the nose will have to use autografts – the patient’s own bone tissue. In my practice, the plastic surgeon does not use implants made of artificial materials, since there is a risk rejection by the body of the foreign bodies.

The best “source” of bone tissue for transplantation is a cartilaginous region of the nasal septum.

If it has already been used during the first operation, then the desired material is taken from the patient’s cartilage of the ear. Rhinoplasty of nose bridge allows to take the bone tissue from the rib or from the ulna.

Places of taking transplants are sutured with a cosmetic suture and scars are almost invisible.

Boston rhinoplasty is implies a little discomfort caused by the presence in the nasal passages of tampons in the postoperative period, which will be removed in the second or third day after nose job.

A cast bandage is superimposed for a period of seven to ten days. This period is sufficient to postoperative edema passed.

The complete disappearance of edematous processes associated with small compaction subcutaneous layers of the nose requires a little more time. Bruises under the eyes resolves at the end of two weeks, and the final result of rhinoplasty nose is reached 2 to 6 months according to the degree of surgical intervention.
