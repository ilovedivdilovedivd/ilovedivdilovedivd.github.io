Is the healing time for a closed Rhinoplasty the same as an open Rhinoplasty?
Open vs. Closed Rhinoplasty healing time
Jul 18, 2014
I usually say healing for open rhinoplasty is only about 2 days more than a closed rhinoplasty due to the stitches. As for long term healing, they are the same.Good luck with your surgery!Warm regards,Dr. Moynihan
I have no dorsal hump, am I a candidate for Rhinoplasty?
No hump, no problem
Jun 17, 2014
Although, sometimes the most common condition, you do not need to have a dorsal hump to be a candidate for rhinoplasty. Many patients have rhinoplasty to decrease the width of their nose, amongst other conditions. I would recommend consulting with a facial plastic surgeon that offers computer imaging so you can visualize your results of a more narrow bridge.
Will the Summer be enough time to heal from rhinoplasty?
Summer Rhinoplasty, healing
May 27, 2014
Many of my patients have the same time frame and questions that you have and I can confidently say 8 weeks after a primary rhinoplasty, your nose should be “better” than your pre-op nose. Every patient heals differently, but each week gets better and better and ultimately gets you closer to your final result. Good luck!George T. Moynihan, M.D.

This can definitely be corrected by elevating the tip with wither the lateral crural overlay technique or lateral crural transposition. Are Asymmetrical Nostrils After Rhinoplasty Normal?
Nostril asymmetry
Feb 28, 2014
Nostril asymmetry following rhinoplasty surgery is often exaggerated by swelling. Once a significant amount of the swelling has resolved (6-12 weeks), the asymmetry can be be evaluated. If still aesthetically upsetting there are procedures that can improve the asymmetry. You should consult with your surgeon to discuss possible solutions. Best of Luck.
