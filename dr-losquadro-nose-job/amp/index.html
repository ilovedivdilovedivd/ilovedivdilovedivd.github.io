Rhinoplasty to rotate tip of nose downwards?
Rotating the tip downward in Asian rhinoplasty
Dec 10, 2014
Asian patients seeking rhinoplasty often desire a more narrow, refined, and projected tip. The tip may also be turned up or overrotated such that nostril show on the frontal view is excessive. Tip grafting can turn down or counterrotate the nose and reduce nostril visibility.
How long does an Asian rhinoplasty last that requires autologous cartilage graft?
Durability of grafts in Asian rhinoplasty
Dec 10, 2014
A large volume of grafting material is often required in Asian rhinoplasty to raise the bridge and refine the tip. Enough septal cartilage is rarely available and rib grafting is often used. There is virtually no resorption of rib cartilage over time so it is essentially permanent.

Implant removal after Asian rhinoplasty
Dec 10, 2014
The goal of most Asian rhinoplasties is to raise the bridge and narrow/refine the tip. This is an “augmentation” rhinoplasty where the nasal framework is left intact and built upon with synthetic implants or grafts. Although after removal it will take time for the stretched nasal skin to accommodate to the nasal skeleton, removal should eventually restore the nose back to its original pliability.
