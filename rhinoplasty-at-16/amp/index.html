Rhinoplasty at 16

Rhinoplasty can be performed at 16 years of age. But are you an appropriate candidate?

Rhinoplasty can be performed at 16 years of age. But are you an appropriate candidate? Good candidates for rhinoplasty have concerns that their surgeon feel can be addressed with surgery.

Further, good candidates for rhinoplasty have reasonable expectations of the potential results. With regard to your age, by itself, you’re a perfectly suitable candidate.

I suggest you visit an experience Facial Plastic Surgeon for full evaluation to determine if rhinoplasty would be helpful for you. (Stephen Weber, MD, FACS, Denver Facial Plastic Surgeon)

As noted below 16 is an appropriate age to consider rhinoplasty as the nose is nearly fully developed at that time (David E. Kim, MD, Beverly Hills Plastic Surgeon)

Sixteen Can Be the Right Age for Rhinoplasty

In girls, most nasal growth is considered complete by age sixteen. So, if your emotional maturity matches your physical, then you certainly can safely get your nose done. Your prospective surgeon will evaluate your candidacy, and if he, your folks and you are all on the same page, then go for it! (James Bartels, MD, Manchester Facial Plastic Surgeon)

What is the earliest age for having rhinoplasty?

Ideally, rhinoplasty should be done once the nose has finished growing. In females, that is typically at age 16 and in males perhaps closer to age 17.

Other factors such a psychological readiness should also be considered. (Maurice M. Khosh, MD, New York Facial Plastic Surgeon)

Rhinoplasty at Age 16

Rhinoplasty is appropriate in a 16 year old physically mature girl. I do surgery on many young ladies at this age before they move on to their university education. In the United States a 16 year old needs parental permission. (Richard W. Fleming, MD, Beverly Hills Facial Plastic Surgeon)

Lower Age Limit Female Rhinoplasty

You should be fine making plans to have a rhinoplasty at your age. The reality is that your nose is very close to being fully developed from a surgical standpoint. The concern when you are younger is that your nose will either continue to grow after having a rhinoplasty or that you would stunt the growth in some way that might be detrimental. I happen to perform rhinoplasty all the time on 16 year females. Males should wait about 1-2 years longer before considering rhinoplasty surgery. (John M. Hilinski, MD, San Diego Facial Plastic Surgeon)

Too Young for Rhinoplasty at 16?

At 16 you are nearly if not completely developed. It’s fine to have a Rhinoplasty at this age. Go for it. You will love your new nose. (Todd B. Koch, MD, Buffalo Plastic Surgeon)
