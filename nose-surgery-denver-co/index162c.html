Nose Surgery In Denver Colorado

Rhinoplasty in Denver CO is a surgical correction of the nose, which is carried out in order to change or restore the shape of the nose and the nasal septum.

The imperfection of nose configuration, its deformation, or a disproportionate size, can become a cause of psychological discomfort and disturbance respiratory function.

There is only one way to solve the problem once and for all.

Rhinoplasty can be performed after prior consultation with the ENT doctor or plastic surgeon.

Indications for rhinoplasty:

a) The deformation of the nose (congenital or posttraumatic);
b) Deviation nasal septum;
c) The desire of the patient to correct a cosmetic defect of the nose, adjust its shape and configuration;
d) The need for reconstruction of post-traumatic nose defects.

Contraindications

a) Coagulation disorders;
b) Acute respiratory infections;
c) Chronic cardiac disease, respiratory and endocrine organs in the stage of decompensation;
d) Inflammatory processes in the area of operations;
e) Age up to 18 years.

The doctor assigns the necessary tests to exclude possible contraindications and conducts a detailed survey before you schedule the surgery.

Types of surgical approaches.

1. Open access.

Open access provides a good overview of the area of operations, allows the correction the most complex defects and the most accurate prediction of the final result after nose job in Denver.

Open access provides a broad overview of the cartilaginous framework of the nose and its other internal structures. An incision is made through the narrowest portion of columella – the skin of the nasal septum, where then the scar remains.

Tip rhinoplasty in Denver can be performed with open access with high accuracy, if necessary its reconstruction, and many other surgical interventions.

2. Closed access.

Beauty Nose Surgery Colorado To Improve Its Aesthetic Appearance

With this access is very limited a visual control all the manipulations in the area of operation. This makes the results of rhinoplasty a less predictable. However, postoperative scars are not visible with closed access , as the incisions performed on the inner surface of the nose.

There are several common variants of closed access:

  1. Interchondral access is used in operations where necessary to isolate the correction of the nasal bridge, including its cartilaginous part.
  2. Alar boundary access is used when necessary the correction of the front of the nose bridge without correction back of the nose.
  3. Septum access can be combined with one of the above methods and used to extend and review of the operated area.
