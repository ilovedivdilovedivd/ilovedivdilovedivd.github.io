Do Breast Reconstruction Tissue Expanders Cause Pain As They Expand?
Do Breast Reconstruction Tissue Expanders Cause Pain As They Expand?
May 23, 2018
It really varies from patient to patient how much discomfort they experience with tissue expanders. Most patients find that the initial expansions are uncomfortable but that this decreases over time. Recently some reconstruction can be done over the muscle with tissue expanders (so called pre-pectoral reconstruction) and those patents seem to have less pain. As with all reconstructive options there are trade-offs to each approach.

Is there a weight limit to breast implants?
Weight limit for implants
Sep 11, 2017
It is unclear what you mean by weight. There is no body weight limit for the placement of implants. The determination of the size of the implant (that is how many cc the implant is) is based on an anatomical factors such as the width of the breast, the amount of breast tissue, stretch of the breast tissue and sometimes the nipple position. These factors can impact the selection of the implant and what size is chosen.

What implants should I get? smooth or textured?
Textured implants
Sep 3, 2017
Textured implants are used when the implant is placed below the gland but on top of the muscle. They are also used on all of the shaped anatomic implants to try and prevent rotation and malposition. Smooth implants are used commonly under the muscle although they can be used over the muscle as well. I am not aware of any compelling data that one should use textured under the muscle. 
What happens if you don’t wear a binder after a TT?
Binder use
Sep 3, 2017
All surgeons have their own specific post-operative routines, I have not found that a binder has any effect on the final post-operative results. We have the patient wear it for comfort. Most patients like to use it for 3 to 4 weeks. In theory it might help prevent seromas but I don’t think anyone has ever proven that.
