Assuming your teeth are cavity free, gums are healthy, and you have appropriate root lengths…

Do I need a gum lift and four porcelain veneers?
Gum Reduction + Veneers
Feb 14, 2022
A doctor would need to do a full exam and review your Xrays to make any clinical recommendations. Based off of the photograph, it looks like a gumlift and veneers maybe a really nice option to enhance your smile. A gumlift must be performed correctly to be permanent and prevent the tissue from growing back– there are different types. If the doctor just used a laser to remove some gum tissue, this is not a permanent procedure. You will want a permanent gumlift before the veneers are placed.
Am I a good candidate for a gum lift?
Likely
Feb 14, 2022
Based off the photo, it looks like you could likely be a good candidate, but this could NOT be determined without a full gum and bone analysis and review of your X-rays. If you are a good candidate, a gum lift will be complete transformative for your smile!
Removing veneers and starting Invisalign instead – possible?
No
Feb 14, 2022
Once there are veneers on the teeth, they will need to have some type of covering on them. You can do Invisalign WITH the veneers if you like, though. Teeth can still be moved with veneers. Once they have had something bonded to the surface, they cannot be left unexposed.
Can Invisalign fix a prominent gummy smile and misaligned teeth?
Partially
Feb 14, 2022
Invisalign can fix the tooth position. The fix for a gummy smile depends on the cause of it… invisalign will not fix this gummy smile based on the amount of gum display. To fix this gummy smile, you would want a gum lift after the invisalign and likely a small amount of botox to drop the lip slightly. This is a very transformative set of procedures that can make a big difference in creating a glamorous smile!
Can my tooth be more aligned and elongated?
Depends
Feb 14, 2022
Moving the tooth orthodonticaly depends on the roots and bone… this is information that is only known by viewing an x-ray. You would need to have a formal evaluation by a dentist to answer this.
