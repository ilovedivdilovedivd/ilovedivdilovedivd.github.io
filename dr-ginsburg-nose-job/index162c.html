Is a rib cartilage rhinoplasty hard to revise or remove after 1 year?
rib cartilage revision
Jun 3, 2019
Thanks for your question.  Revision rhinoplasty is more challenging than primary rhinoplasty in general, the removal of cartilage itself is no particularly difficult, although repairing the issue you are having may be.  See someone who is experienced with revision rhinoplasty.  Best of luck!
I had a rhinoplasty/septoplasty. Can this be fixed?
Can this be fixed
Apr 24, 2019
I think that a revision is definitely something to consider given how much this sounds like it is bothering you.  From the picture it appears you may have some issues with the bony portion of your nose and a good intranasal exam would be required to fully evaluate the cause of your breathing.  I would discuss with your original surgeon and consider evaluation with someone experienced with rhinoplasty in general but specifically revision cases.  This should be bale to be improved.  Best of luck.
Can nose job related cartilage bump be corrected?
bumps after rhinoplasty
Apr 22, 2019
Hello and thanks for your question, you appear to have thin skin.  It is not uncommon to see minor imperfections after rhinoplasty with this type of skin, the bumps can be removed or smoothed out with a revision procedure, you may also need something place under the skin to camouflage such as fascia or crushed cartilage to help smooth things out.  Hope this helps!
Should I be concerned that I have not seen before and after photos of my doctor’s work? How do I move
Before and Afters
Apr 16, 2019
Before and after photos for rhinoplasty are a must, not only will you get an idea of what you can expect for an outcome, but you will also get an idea of how often the doctor does rhinoplasty.  Have specifically asked about before and afters, if so and you have not been shown any I would consider getting some other opinions.  If he/she does not have before and after photos, chnaces are they don’t perform the procedure often.
Nose speculum used after rhinoplasty – Will this check up affect the result of my surgery ?
Nose speculum
Mar 30, 2019
I commonly will use a nasal speculum to examine the nose even less that 6 mo after I do a rhinoplasty. I can’t imagine that you would have anything to worry about with this. At 6 mo the structures in your nose should be pretty well established and should not be able to be disrupted from a routine exam. Hope this helps!
