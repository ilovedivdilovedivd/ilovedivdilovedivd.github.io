Statement: Dr. Timothy M. Greco is a double board-certified facial plastic surgeon in Bala Cynwyd, Pennsylvania, near Philadelphia. His practice focuses solely on plastic and reconstructive surgery of the face, head, and neck, including surgical and nonsurgical rhinoplasty.

“I love rhinoplasty,” says Dr. Grego. “With my training in head and neck surgery and facial plastic surgery, I’ve taken the nose apart and put it back together. With that knowledge, I have a good understanding of what will allow my patients to have an aesthetically pleasing nose that functions the way they want it to function.” Dr. Grego is known as a meticulous and talented surgeon with an eye for creating beautiful results.

Dr. Grego received his bachelor’s degree in biology from Lafayette College. After earning his medical degree at Hahnemann University School of Medicine, he completed a surgical internship at Lehigh Valley Hospital Center, followed by a residency in head and neck surgery at Thomas Jefferson University Hospital in Philadelphia.

While Dr. Greco is known for his expertise in rhinoplasty, he is adept at numerous other facial plastic surgery procedures. From facelifts and brow lifts to otoplasty (ear surgery) and blepharoplasty (eyelid surgery), Dr. Greco strives to achieve optimal, personalized results for each of his patients.

He also offers minimally invasive procedures such as Botox, dermal fillers, and Kybella to rejuvenate his patients’ appearance without surgery.

Known as an experienced thought leader, Dr. Greco has written numerous papers on facial plastic surgery. He also regularly instructs physicians on groundbreaking techniques. His commitment to excellence and professionalism explains why he has been honored as a “Best of Philly” recipient multiple times by Philadelphia Style magazine.

Dr. Grego is board-certified by the American Board of Facial Plastic and Reconstructive Surgery and the American Board of Otolaryngology–Head and Neck Surgery. He is a member of the American Academy of Facial Plastic and Reconstructive Surgery and is a fellow of the American College of Surgeons. He is an adjunct assistant professor at the University of Pennsylvania School of Medicine in the department of otolaryngology–head and neck surgery.
