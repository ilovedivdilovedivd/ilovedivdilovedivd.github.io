Rhinoplasty in NJ is an operation with which you can change the shape of the nose, emphasizing the harmony of the individual image.

This surgical procedure involves single-step correction of all portions of the external nose, and if necessary the effects on its internal structure.

Each operation has its own characteristics, and provides a reduction the nasal structure (Reduction rhinoplasty), increasing its projection in certain areas (augmentation rhinoplasty), correction of congenital or acquired deformations (reconstructive rhinoplasty), restoration the quality of breathing (functional rhinoplasty).

The intervention can be performed as an open or closed method.

The anatomical structure of the nose

The anatomical structure of the nose is rather complicated. It is a pyramid constructed from bone and cartilage tissue.

The nose is covered by a skin from outside and inside mucosa. The basic appearance of the nose tip is determined a cartilages of the nose.

Nose bump consists of the lateral cartilage, the nasal bones and the upper edge of the septum cartilage. These structures are often subject to change at the time of reduction rhinoplasty.

Reducing the width of the nose, also includes a number of small external incisions. These approaches allow to carry out all the necessary volume of intervention. At the final stage is performed a tamponade of the nasal cavity and the outer plaster cast is superimposed on the nose.
