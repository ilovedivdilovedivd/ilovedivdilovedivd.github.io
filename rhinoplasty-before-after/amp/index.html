Be Certain You Are Comparing Apples to Apples when Viewing Rhinoplasty Pre/Post Op Photos

Before & after photos should be helpful to you when choosing your surgeon. It is true that surgeons usually show their best results, but if there are a large number of good results shown, it usually means the surgeon does a lot of rhinoplasties and gets a number of good results.

However, there are several other things you should look for to make certain the surgeon is showing you a true representation of his work. The way the photos are presented give you some idea of the way the surgeon goes about this work.

For instance: Most before & after photos are kept in some form of an album. If so, the album should be presented in an attractive, neat, well-organized fashion.

There should be numerous photographs that are at least 4X6″ in size of different types of noses. There should be at least 2 different views of the patient (front & side, front & oblique, side & basal, etc).

Four different views are even better because they give you the most accurate representation of how the patient really looks.

The pre & post op of the same view should have the heads in the same position, the same facial expressions, i.e., both smiling or neither smiling, the same amount of facial makeup and the backgrounds should be the same.

A soft blue or gray background is usually preferred. this allows you to compare apples to apples. Taking good representative photographs is difficult and usually requires a person who has been trained in the art.
